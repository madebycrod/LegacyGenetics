About Massage

What is Canine Massage Therapy?

Canine Massage is a branch of massage therapy that promotes health in dogs. Increased relaxation and pain relief are a few of the benefits.

What is Canine Sports Massage Therapy?

Canine Sports Massage Therapy, while encompassing all the techniques and benefits of common canine massage, goes one step further by isolating and alleviating the problems caused by normal day-to-day living, aging, sporting activities, injury, surgery, lack of muscle tone and sluggish circulation. Canine Sports Massage Therapy promotes better fitness and overall health while helping to keep your dog in their best physical shape.

Some Benefits of Canine Sports Massage Therapy:

Improves flexibility and range of joint motion which helps prevent injury

Eases pain due to arthritis, muscle overuse/underuse, muscle spasm, surgeries and injuries

Reduces anxiety

Boosts immunity

Relieves muscle and joint pain and stress

Increases athletic performance

Promotes healing and increases blood and lymph circulation

Reduces scar tissue formation and assists in recovery after surgery or injury

Toenail Clipping

The benefits of well-trimmed toenails in a dog go way beyond aesthetics. Long toenails can wreak havoc with the natural alignment of the feet and legs causing joint stress, instability, poor posture, altered gait, and arthritis. Properly trimmed toenails allow for a dog’s correct foot placement on the ground, providing a natural and comfortable stance, which creates good posture and gait. The importance of well-trimmed toenails cannot be overstated. We’ll work with you and your dog to get the nails to a proper length and keep them there. And yes, we’ll be happy to teach you how to trim your own dog’s nails!

Ear Cleaning

Dog’s ears differ from human ears in that dogs have mostly vertical ear canals while humans have horizontal canals. Because of this, it’s easy for moisture and debris to get stuck in the dog’s ear canal which can lead to itchy and sometimes smelly ears. A regular, gentle cleaning with ear solution can reduce the chance of irritation or infection and help keep your dog happy and smelling good!

Anal Gland Expressing

Anal glands are two sacs located on either side of a dog’s anus. These glands produce an unpleasant smelling fluid whose sole purpose is to lend a unique scent to their stool, which helps other dogs to identify them. Unfortunately, there are times when the anal glands don’t empty (express) naturally and the fluid builds up inside the sac. Left unattended, the sac can rupture and a trip to the vet is in order. The symptoms of anal gland problems can be scooting across the floor or twirling in a sitting position, biting at the area, or sometimes, you’ll just notice a strange and unpleasant odor. Some dogs never have trouble with their anal glands while others may have occasional or frequent problems. We don’t recommend routine anal gland expressing if you or your vet are not seeing signs of a problem. But if there is a problem, we’ll be happy to express the glands for you – outside, of course!

Teeth and Gum Assessment and Care

The condition of your dog’s teeth and gums can be a window to their health. Bad breath, inflamed gums, plaque and tartar, and broken or cracked teeth, can all be a sign of trouble brewing. Kidney disease and heart valve inflammation are more prevalent in dogs with periodontal disease. Unfortunately, a large percentage of dogs have some sort of periodontal disease by the time they’re 3 years old. We can inspect the state of your dog’s teeth and gums, perform basic dental hygiene and help you decide on a dental care plan that works for you and your dog.

We’ll take the time to do the job right and we’ll help your dog become accustomed to the feel of the brush. Before long, your dog will be begging for more!

Healthy Stretching, Flexibility and Range of Motion Exercises

Range of Motion (ROM) is defined as “the extent of movement of a joint”. Good flexibility is necessary for a joint to have full ROM. Healthy stretching increases your dog’s flexibility, thereby increasing range of motion. Stretching and flexibility also increase circulation and send more oxygen to the muscle which helps create a stronger muscle and joint. Strong muscles and joints are less prone to injury and are less susceptible to the stiffness and pain that is associated with aging. Here are some of the many benefits your dog will enjoy from healthy stretching and range of motion exercises:

  • Alleviates arthritis and minimizes the pain
  • Increases blood and lymph circulation (the lymphatic system is part of the immune system)
  • Reduces inflammation
  • Helps prevent soft tissue injuries like tendonitis
  • Reduces back pain caused by tight muscles
  • Helps to regain muscle strength and encourages muscle and joint resiliency after injury or surgery
  • Creates a calm demeanor in your dog and feels just plain good!

This service includes a short warm-up walk. In the event your vet has prescribed movement restrictions, a gentle massage or warm damp towel application will be substituted for the walk once we get clearance from your vet.

Healthy stretching and range of motion exercises are also included in the Canine Revival Certified Massage routine. Massage helps keep muscles toned, improves circulation and lymphatic drainage, and loosens the muscles around joints, promoting ease of movement. It can be especially helpful in easing the stiffness of arthritis, enabling your dog to maintain an active lifestyle.

Exercise and Conditioning

If you have a senior dog facing the normal aging process, or a dog that delights in being a couch potato, you may have noticed some muscle loss and lethargy. Or maybe your dog has experienced an injury, illness or surgery that requires body reconditioning exercises. Regardless of the circumstances, we can help your dog!

The Senior or Inactive Dog:

The process of aging can cause our dogs to become more sedentary which results in loss of muscle mass and balance. This holds true for the less active younger dog too. As muscle tone diminishes there is a higher probabiliy of injury from trips, falls, and sprains. Being sedentary can lead to weight gain and puts added stress on joints. It also causes poor circulation which affects the organs and processes in the body. We can design conditioning exercises specifically for your dog to help promote better muscle tone, strength and health.

The Recuperating Dog:

We’ll work directly with your veterinarian as they develop a plan for your dog’s recuperation. We can assist in a multi-faceted approach to help your dog recover by using specialized instability training to encourage balance, independent limb awareness, and build core strength.

Whether you have an aging dog, a couch potato or a dog recovering from a set-back, these exercises will help your dog get back on track!

Behavioral Counseling

Sometimes, our perfect pooch may display a behavior that both puzzles and upsets us. Typical behavior problems include growling, barking, lunging at people and other dogs, destructive behaviors like digging and chewing things up, the fear of loud or unfamiliar things or people, and separation anxiety. Changing your dog’s behavior takes some patience, time and a strong commitment from you. Our goal is to help you find the cause of your dog’s undesirable behavior, understand it, and take steps to improve, eliminate, or redirect the unwanted behavior into one that is more acceptable.

Obedience Training

Obedience training should be FUN! - fun for the dog and fun for the handler. No dog is too young or too old to learn basic obedience. Puppies as young as 8 weeks of age can learn how to “sit”, “down” and “come” with very short, but frequent, training sessions. Older dogs can learn at an even more rapid pace and will enjoy the extra attention and time they get to spend with you! There are so many benefits and advantages to obedience training that is done correctly. Some people think obedience training will break a dog’s spirit but, in reality, it helps build a wonderful bond and relationship between you and your dog! A dog, properly trained, is a joy to have along on outings and has far fewer temperament and behavior problems than a dog that is raised without rules. If you are unable to obedience train your dog due to physical or other concerns, we’ll be happy to train your dog with you. If you prefer to be the trainer, we’ll show you positive training methods that produce fast and lasting results! We’ll make obedience training FUN! for you and your dog!
